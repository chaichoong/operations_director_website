The framework

OPTIMISE.
Eight pillars.
One operating system.

Every Operations Director build follows the OPTIMISE framework. It's the sequence we've refined across dozens of SME installations — the order matters, because fixing the wrong thing first costs you twice.

O

Organise

Map the business as it is. Every process, tool, bottleneck and owner. You can't systemise what you haven't seen clearly.

P

Prioritise

Rank every leak by cost and effort. We fix the 20% of problems causing 80% of the damage before anything else.

T

Templatise

Turn every recurring decision into a documented SOP. If it happens more than twice, it becomes a template.

I

Integrate

Connect the stack. Airtable, Make.com, Slack, Gmail, Google Workspace, Claude. One flow of data, not five.

M

Measure

Build the dashboards that matter. Cash position, pipeline health, team capacity, compliance status. Real numbers, updated live.

I

Improve

Weekly check-ins against objectives. What moved, what didn't, what needs fixing this week.

S

Systemise

Remove the founder from the workflow wherever possible. Automation first, delegation second, founder decision last.

E

Elevate

The founder shifts from operator to owner. Strategy, growth and the next bet — not the day-to-day.

Done in order, OPTIMISE turns a founder-dependent business into a founder-optional one.

That's the whole point.

Questions

The things founders actually want to know.

Straight answers on pricing, refunds, data safety, and how this differs from the template kits you've already burned money on.

Question 01 · Pricing
How much does this cost?
+
Full OS builds start from £10,000, scoped by complexity and team size. We run a Systems Audit first at £497 — credited in full against any build you commission within 30 days.
Question 02 · Timeline
How long does an installation take?
+
A single module is typically 2–4 weeks. A full eight-module OS is 3–6 months, built in prioritised phases so you're seeing value from week two, not month six.
Question 03 · Stack
Do I need to already use Airtable, Make.com or Slack?
+
No. We'll either build on your existing stack or install ours. If you're starting fresh, we set up the accounts, licences and architecture. You own everything from day one.
Question 04 · Ownership
What happens after handover? Am I on my own?
+
You own the system outright. No lock-in. We offer optional ongoing support retainers, but most clients run independently after a 30-day handover period. Every SOP, automation and dashboard is fully documented for your team.
Question 05 · Data safety
Is my data safe?
+
Yes. Your data lives in your accounts, not ours. We build on platforms with enterprise-grade security (Airtable, Google Workspace, Make.com). We sign NDAs as standard and never retain client data after handover.
Question 06 · vs Templates
How is this different from buying a template or a course?
+
Templates give you a blank system and a manual. Courses give you theory. We build the working system inside your business, connected to your actual tools, data and team. You don't configure anything. You use it from day one.
Question 07 · Fit
What size business is this for?
+
Founder-led SMEs with 1–50 employees. If you're a solo founder drowning in admin, a full module fits. If you're a 30-person team with no documented systems, you need the full OS. Under 1 employee or over 50, we're probably not the right fit.
Question 08 · Refunds
Do you offer refunds?
+
Yes. If you're not satisfied with the audit deliverable, we refund it in full — no questions. For builds, we work in phased milestones with clear deliverables at each stage. If we miss a milestone, you don't pay for it.
Question 09 · Technical skill
Do I need technical skills to use the system after handover?
+
No. Everything is built for non-technical operators. Your team uses Slack, fills in Airtable forms, and reads dashboards. The technical layer — Make.com scenarios, API integrations, AI prompts — is hidden and maintained by documented SOPs.
Question 10 · Flexibility
What if my business changes after the OS is built?
+
Systems should flex with the business. Every module is built to be modified — by us on retainer, by a freelancer, or by a capable team member following our documentation. You're never stuck with a system that no longer fits.
Question 11 · Integrations
Can you integrate with tools I already use?
+
In most cases, yes. We regularly integrate Gmail, Google Workspace, GoHighLevel, Xero, QuickBooks, Stripe, HubSpot, Pipedrive and dozens of others via Make.com. If a tool has an API, we can usually connect it.
Question 12 · Trust
Why should I trust you to build this?
+
Because I run the same infrastructure inside my own businesses — a multi-property portfolio, a consultancy, and a charity brand — every day. We don't sell theory. We sell what we use. Book the audit and see for yourself.
